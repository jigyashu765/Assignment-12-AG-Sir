Ramsila pahad

Ramsila Pahad, also known as Ramshila Hill, is a sacred pilgrimage site located near the city of Gaya in the Indian state of Bihar. The hill is situated on the banks of the river Phalgu and is believed to have a great religious significance among the Hindus and Jains. The hill is also believed to have played an important role in the life of Lord Rama and Sita, and it is considered a sacred site for the devotees of Lord Rama.

According to the Hindu mythology, it is believed that Lord Rama and Sita, during their exile, visited the hill and stayed here for a while. It is believed that Lord Rama performed a yajna (fire sacrifice) at this place and established a Shiva Lingam, which is still worshipped by the devotees. The hill is also believed to be the place where Lord Rama and Sita worshipped Lord Shiva before returning to Ayodhya. Therefore, Ramsila Pahad is considered a sacred site among the devotees of Lord Rama.

It is believed that Lord Mahavira, the last Tirthankara of Jainism, spent a significant amount of time here during his life. According to the Jain beliefs, it is believed that Lord Mahavira achieved his enlightenment under a tree located near the hill. The tree is still present at the site and is worshipped by the Jains. The hill is also believed to be the place where Lord Mahavira gave his first sermon after achieving enlightenment. Therefore, the hill is considered a sacred site among the Jains as well.
